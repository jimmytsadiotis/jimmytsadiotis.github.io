Experience

  1. Security Engineer | DevSecOps @ Gcore

    Sept 2022 — Present

    As a Security Engineer at Gcore, I am responsible for enhancing the organization's security posture through proactive vulnerability management and the implementation of DevSecOps practices. My key responsibilities and contributions include:

    • • Leading Vulnerability Management: Spearheading vulnerability management efforts, ensuring the timely identification, assessment, and remediation of security weaknesses across Gcore's extensive systems and infrastructure. This involves utilizing vulnerability scanning tools, prioritizing findings based on risk, and coordinating remediation activities with relevant teams.
    • • Ad-hoc Security Assessments: Conducting focused security assessments in response to specific requests or emerging security requirements. These assessments leverage my expertise in current attack vectors, OWASP guidelines, and industry best practices to provide actionable security insights.
    • • Attack Surface Reduction Program: Initiating and driving the attack surface reduction program to minimize potential entry points for threats. This includes identifying and implementing security controls, hardening systems, and continuously monitoring for new exposures.
    • • Penetration Testing Coordination: Managing the full lifecycle of penetration testing engagements, from initial scoping and planning to overseeing execution and meticulously reviewing findings reports. I ensure that penetration tests are effective in simulating real-world attacks and providing valuable insights into security posture.
    • • DevSecOps Optimization: Developing and advocating for improvements and action plans to strengthen security within DevOps environments and CICD pipelines. This includes promoting security automation, integrating security tools into the development process, and fostering a security-conscious culture within engineering teams.
    • • Software Composition Analysis (SCA): Taking ownership of ongoing software composition analysis to thoroughly evaluate open-source components and dependencies used in software projects. This includes identifying potential vulnerabilities in dependencies, ensuring license compliance, and mitigating risks associated with open-source software.

  2. Senior Security Consultant @ Grant Thornton

    Oct 2019 — Sept 2022

    As a Senior Security Consultant at Grant Thornton, I delivered cybersecurity services to a diverse portfolio of global clients, focusing on both technical assessments and advisory services. My responsibilities encompassed:

    • • Penetration Testing: Leading and executing infrastructure and web application penetration testing projects for clients across various industries. This involved utilizing a range of penetration testing tools and techniques to identify vulnerabilities and assess security controls.
    • • Security Awareness Assessments (Social Engineering): Designing and conducting security awareness assessments using social engineering tactics to evaluate the effectiveness of client security awareness programs and identify areas for improvement in employee security behaviors.
    • • Vulnerability Communication & Remediation Guidance: Clearly communicating complex technical vulnerabilities to both technical developers and non-technical management audiences. Providing actionable and practical remediation steps to clients to effectively address identified security weaknesses.
    • • IS Audits & Compliance: Actively participating in the preparation and execution of Information Security (IS) audits based on recognized industry standards such as ISO 27001 and PCI DSS NIST frameworks.
    • • Cybersecurity Awareness Academy: Conceptualizing, developing, and leading Grant Thornton (Cyprus)'s Cybersecurity Awareness academy. This initiative involved creating training content, delivering workshops, and promoting cybersecurity awareness within the organization and to clients.

  3. Research Assistant @ Glasgow Cyber Defence Group

    Jun 2019 — Sep 2019

    As a Research Assistant at the Glasgow Cyber Defence Group, I contributed to research focused on enhancing Industrial Control System (ICS) security through innovative discovery methods. My contributions included:

    • • ICS Discovery Method Development: Developing and implementing novel communication protocol-based methods for the discovery of ICS devices within complex network environments. This research aimed to improve the visibility and monitoring capabilities of security professionals in industrial settings.
    • • Safety Impact Assessment of Network Discovery Tools: Conducting thorough assessments of the potential safety impacts associated with the use of network discovery tools in ICS environments. This critical research focused on ensuring that security tools could be safely deployed without disrupting critical industrial operations or compromising safety standards.
